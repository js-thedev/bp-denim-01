How to Properly Treat Your Denim

Maintain the quality and longevity of your denim.

Washing Your Denim

General rule of thumb is to wash after every 10 wears unless it smells or has a severe stain.

The washing process described here focuses on minimizing impacts on the denim and the clothes you wash with your denim.

1. Button Up

Keep the buttons in place, prevent snagging and damage other clothes.

2. Turn Inside Out

Minimize contact, prevent unintended fades on denim

3. Wash With Similar Colors

Prevent indigo transfer to other clothes.

4. No Overloading

Overloading the washing machine while washing your denim could leave vertically folded trace on denim.

5. Mild Detergent, No Softners

Softners shortens the lifespan of the fabric.

6. Cold Water

Hot water will expedite the color loss.

Drying Your Denim

Always line dry in shade to dry your denim. Using artificial heat to dry the moisture out of any piece of clothing will shorten the lifespan of the fabric. Unless it is absolutely necessary (i.e. you are at hotel and you need to checkout the next day), avoid using dryer and let the water dry out by itself.

Storing Your Denim

You would always want to avoid direct sunlight when storing your denim. Sun is strong enough to bleach the color away from your clothes.

Right after washing and drying, you can put your denim in your closet. When you're storing your denim in between your wears, put it on a hanger and leave some room around it. This way, natural ventilation will buy you some time before your denim starts to smell.
